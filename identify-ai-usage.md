# How to Identify AI-assisted Writing Patterns

When exploring a student's writing process report, teachers can use the pointers below to identify potential AI-assisted writing.

---


### 1. Inspect active typing time in the Typing Time section

Learn more about the Typing Time section <a href="https://help.processfeedback.org/?insight=typing-time" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">here</a>.

### 2. Inspect large "external" paste events in the Paste Events section

A student may paste text generated by AI into their editor during their writing process. Here is how to identify such paste events:

1. In the Paste Events section of the report, click on the "Characters" column to sort, so that large paste events appear on top.
2. Look for rows that have "Likely from Outside" in the "Source" column.
3. Click on "Pasted Text" in the row and inspect if the text appears to be from outside source.
4. Click on the "Time of Paste" to open Compare Changes and inspect where in the editor this text was pasted.

Learn more about the Paste Events section <a href="https://help.processfeedback.org/?insight=copy-paste-events" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">here</a>.

### 3. Inspect text deletions in the Text Added and Removed section

Check how often deletions took place and what was deleted in the Text Added Removed section.

Learn more about the Text Added and Removed section <a href="https://help.processfeedback.org/?insight=text-added-removed" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">here</a>.

### 5. Inspect free typing and revision patterns in the Edited Sentences section

Learn more about the Edited Sentences section <a href="https://help.processfeedback.org/?insight=edited-sentences" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">here</a>.

### 6. Increase the resolution when viewing the report

By default Process Feedback only picks up a maximum of 360 time points from the editing history. This is relatively enough detail for reports with typing time less than 15 or 20 minutes. However, if you want a more granular view, open the Playback or other report sections and choose a more detailed option in the “Level of details” dropdown.
