# How to Identify AI-assisted Writing Patterns 

---

When exploring a student's Writing Process Report, teachers can use the pointers below to identify potential AI-assisted writing. 

**1. Inspect active typing time in the [Typing Time](https://help.processfeedback.org//?insight=typing-time) section**  




**2. Inspect large "external" paste events in the [Paste Events section]((https://help.processfeedback.org//?insight=copy-paste-events))**  

A student may paste text generated by AI into their editor during their writing process. Here is how to identify such paste events:
1. In the Paste Events section of the report, click on the "Characters" column to sort, so that large paste events appear on top.
2. Look for rows that have "Likely from Outside" in the "Source" column.
3. Click on "Pasted Text" in the row and inspect if the text appears to be from outside source.
4. Click on the "Time of Paste" to open Compare Changes and inspect where in the editor this text text was pasted.

You can learn more about the Paste Events section [here].

**3. Inspect text deletions in the [Text Added and Removed section](https://help.processfeedback.org//?insight=text-added-removed)**  

Check how often deletions took place and what was deleted in the Text Added Removed section.

You can learn more about the Text Added and Removed section [here].

**4. Inspect breaks taken in the [Active Typing Sessions section](https://help.processfeedback.org//?insight=active-typing-sessions-chart-view)**  


You can learn more about the Active Typing Sessions section [here].

**5. Inspect free typing and revision patterns in the [Edited Sentences section](https://help.processfeedback.org//?insight=edited-sentences)**


You can learn more about the Edited Sentences section [here].




**6. Increase the resolution when viewing report**

By default Process Feedback only picks up maximum 360 time points from the editing history. This is relatively enough detail for reports with typing time less than 15 or 20 minutes. However, if you are are interested in a more granular view, in Playback and other report sections, when opening report choose more detailed options in "Level of details" dropdown.
