
# Create an Assignment in Process Feedback

---

In this approach, Process Feedback functions as a mini but independent learning management system (LMS), allowing you to create a question, collect student submissions, and review all responses in a single dashboard.

Creating an assignment with a submission deadline takes just a few steps. This process generates a unique link that students can use to access the assignment and submit their responses. When students open the link, the assignment appears at the top of the editor. After completing the task, students can review their report and click “Submit” to finalize their work. As submissions come in, you can track and manage them all from a single dashboard.

You or your students do not need to create an account to use Process Feedback.

Using Process Feedback in a class involves two key steps. First, inform your students in advance about what to expect. Next, provide them with instructions on how to use it.

### Step 1. Inform students in advance—don’t surprise them 

Students should be informed in advance that you are interested not only in their final product but also in their working process. To do this, include a note in your course syllabus or send an announcement at the beginning of the semester. Here is an [example classroom announcement](./classroom-announcement.md).

### Step 2. Create an assignment and share the link with your students

First create an assignment [here](https://app.processfeedback.org/createassignment). Students must use the editor link you provided them for their writing.

When you create an assignment, you will see instructions that you can share with you students.

### Step 3. Try the submission link yourself

After creating an assignment, try the submission link yourself once and test if your submission appears in the teacher dashboard.
